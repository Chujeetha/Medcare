Vitamin-D

A human body produces vitamin D as a response to sun exposure. Vitamin D is essential for several reasons, including maintaining healthy bones and teeth. It may also protect against a range of diseases and conditions, such as type 1 diabetes. Supporting immune, brain, and nervous system health regulating insulin levels and supporting diabetes management supporting lung function and cardiovascular health influencing the expression of genes involved in cancer development. Vitamin D is a group of fat-soluble secosteroids responsible for increasing intestinal absorption of calcium, magnesium, and phosphate, and many other biological effects. In humans, the most important compounds in this group are vitamin D₃ and vitamin D₂.
